Vaishnavi acts in a play

Hedda Gabler

Author: Henrik Ibsen

TESMAN.

[Wraps up the slippers and lays them on the table.]
Well, you see, we are well into September now.
HEDDA
[Again restless.]
Yes, to think of it!--already in--in September.
TESMAN.Don't you think Aunt Julia's manner was strange, dear? Almost solemn? Can you imagine what was the matter with her? Eh?
HEDDAI scarcely know her, you see. Is she not often like that?
TESMAN.No, not as she was to-day.
HEDDA.
[Leaving the glass door.]
Do you think she was annoyed about the bonnet?
TESMAN Oh, scarcely at all. Perhaps a little, just at the moment--
